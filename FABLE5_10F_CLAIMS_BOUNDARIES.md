# 10f - Product Positioning and Claims Boundaries

_Fable 5, 2026-07-10. Per D86, Fable's 10f role is limited to defining what may be claimed, what may not, and how to position against the personality-test category. Sonnet 5 writes and polishes the actual landing-page copy inside these boundaries. These rules apply to the landing page first, and to every outward-facing surface (share page, email, card CTA, app store copy later) by extension._

## 1. The one-sentence positioning

Phil OS is a philosophical profiling instrument built to psychometric standards, measuring what you believe rather than how you behave, with a contradiction engine no other instrument offers. Rigor of construction is the claim. Achieved validation is not, yet.

## 2. What MAY be claimed (design and construction claims)

1. **Built to psychometric standards**: reversed-item keying in both directions, balanced item design, structural integrity checks, versioned instrument, documented methodology. These are true statements about construction and process.
2. **Grounded in academic philosophy**: constructs anchored to the real positions in the philosophical literature (PhilPapers is the internal gold standard and may be named), every axis pole a genuine position with real defenders.
3. **Measures beliefs, not behaviour**: the depth contrast with trait tests is real and claimable as a difference in what is measured.
4. **The contradiction engine is unique in kind**: 42 rules, three tiers, refining questions. "We know of no other instrument that does this" is the honest form; "no comparable tool exists anywhere" overreaches into an unverifiable market claim.
5. **Transparency claims**: that the instrument's structure, version history, and scoring approach are documented and consistent. Claimable because a reader could verify it.
6. **A planned validation path**: reliability and item statistics will be computed and published once the completion base reaches statistical adequacy (N >= 300). Stating the plan is honest and differentiating; it signals the standard without claiming the result.

## 3. What may NOT be claimed

1. **"Scientifically validated," "proven," "science-backed results," or any equivalent.** The instrument's own standard forbids statistical claims before N >= 300 real completions. The current live sentence "Personality tests aren't science. Phil OS is." fails this boundary and must be replaced (it is also flagged as launch blocker 5 in the Phase 15 re-audit). The claimable form is construction rigor: built to the standards, publishing the method, validation path stated.
2. **No accuracy percentages, no reliability numbers, no "research shows" about Phil OS itself.** Nothing exists yet to cite.
3. **No Big Five / OCEAN language anywhere** (D94). The instrument does not measure it and the inferred block is gone from the pipeline.
4. **No therapeutic, diagnostic, or mental-health claims.** "Self-insight," "self-understanding," and "sharpen your own reasoning" are the ceiling. Nothing that reads as assessment of psychological health.
5. **No determinism-of-fate phrasing** ("this is who you are forever"). The instrument measures current positions; people revise. This is also a neutrality matter: essentialist framing would side with one pole of two live axes.
6. **No pricing, payment, or "premium" language** until herbeoordeling resolves. Nothing that could read as commercial activity.
7. **No claims about what the respondent's data will scientifically contribute** beyond the actual research program: de-identified, opt-in, default off.

## 4. Positioning against the personality-test category

1. **The comparison must be inferred, not named** (Andre's dictation, audit plan 10f). The current landing page names MBTI, Enneagram, Human Design, and Big Five directly. Replace with category-level language: "the popular tests," "most personality frameworks," "the tests you have already taken." The reader will supply the names themselves, which is the point.
2. The honest category contrast, which is strong enough without embellishment: the popular category was largely never built on validation evidence, and it measures surface traits. Phil OS measures the layer underneath (beliefs, commitments, worldview) and is built openly to the standards the category skipped. Both halves are true and neither requires claiming achieved validation.
3. **Punch up the depth contrast, not the science contrast.** "They measure how you behave at parties. This maps what you believe about reality." That register is claimable, memorable, and safe. The science contrast invites the exact scrutiny we cannot yet survive.
4. The Big Five is a special case: it IS validated psychometrics. Never lump it with MBTI-class tests in any "not science" framing, or the copy is factually wrong in a way any psychology-literate reader will catch. The safe contrast with the Big Five is depth of subject matter (traits vs beliefs), not rigor. Better: avoid naming it at all (rule 4.1 already requires that).

## 5. Contradiction engine framing rules (the premier feature)

1. Frame: it finds the places where your beliefs pull against each other, and asks the questions that help you sharpen your own reasoning. Three levels: hard contradictions, consistency flags, interesting tensions worth examining.
2. **Explicitly not a judgment tool.** The copy must say some version of: a tension is not a failure; complex worldviews carry tensions, and knowing yours is the advantage. This is a hard requirement from Andre's dictation.
3. The C-tier register from D44/D57 extends to marketing: coherent classical positions (the libertarian retributivist case) are named as coherent, never as gotchas the engine "caught."
4. Claimable strength: the engine runs a fixed, inspectable rule set (42 rules), not AI vibes. Determinism of the mechanism is a trust feature; say it.

## 6. Mandatory qualifiers and their placement

1. Beta status: visible on the landing page while the beta QA flag ships with every question.
2. "Not yet independently validated; validation planned and versioned" belongs once, in the methodology/credibility section, stated with confidence rather than apology. Honesty about the validation stage, stated plainly, reads as rigor and differentiates from the category that never states it at all.
3. Free-to-take framing only; no monetisation language (see 3.6).
